### Detailed Analysis of Financial Calculation Logic

#### 1. FOIR Calculation and Salary Dependency

The Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio (FOIR) is the most critical metric across all lenders, determining the maximum debt an applicant can carry relative to their income.

*   **Maximum Limits (70% - 80%):** The highest maximum FOIR limits are reserved for the premium, high-income, and low-risk segments.
    *   **Poonawala (PFL)** offers the absolute highest stated FOIR of **80%** for Super HNI (Net Take Home > â‚¹2.5L) and Super A/Cat A categories within the very-low risk band.
    *   **Aditya Birla Capital Ltd (ABCL)**, **Piramal Finance**, and **Tata Capital (TCL)** cap their standard FOIR at **75%**. TCL markets this 75% as "Best in PL Industry".
    *   **Kotak Mahindra Bank** also allows **75%** FOIR for customers with live Home Loan tracks.

*   **FOIR Increase with Salary:** In almost all cases, the maximum allowable FOIR increases as the net monthly salary (NTH) increases, reflecting the assumption that high earners have greater discretionary income after meeting basic living expenses.
    *   **Bajaj Finserv** limits FOIR for salaried individuals earning < â‚¹50K to **60%**, but allows it to rise to **70%** for those earning > â‚¹1L.
    *   **IDFC First Bank** raises the limit from 50% (Cat C, D, Unlisted, < â‚¹40K salary) to **70%** (for all categories > â‚¹75K salary).
    *   **MAS Finance** provides a very strict, incremental scale, starting at **30%** (â‚¹18Kâ€“â‚¹25K) and only reaching **60%** (â‚¹75K and above).
    *   **TCL** follows this pattern, moving from 60% (NTH < â‚¹50K) up to **75%** (NTH > â‚¹75K).

*   **Exceptions and Compensating Factors:** FOIR calculation is often influenced by housing status and existing relationships:
    *   **BFL** allows a higher FOIR (up to 70%) for customers with an existing Home Loan ("Ever HL in CIBIL Report") compared to those without (65% for the same income bracket).
    *   **ABCL** offers a 100% FOIR cap in their Paperless BT program, provided specific MOB (Months on Book) and track criteria are met.

#### 2. Multiplier Logic

Loan Multipliers (a ratio applied to Net Take Home/NTH) are used by many lenders, usually calculating the loan amount as: *Loan Amount = NTH \* Multiplier*. Some lenders use the multiplier on the *remaining* salary after deducting existing obligations.

*   **Kotak Mahindra Bank's Unique Calculation:** Kotak explicitly uses the formula: **(NTH â€“ Obligation) \* Multiplier**, effectively reducing the loan size based on existing debt before the multiplier is applied. They offer the highest stated maximum multiplier of **32x**.
*   **Tata Capital's Approach:** TCL explicitly states that they take the multiplier on the **Net in hand Salary (NTH)** and **do not deduct existing obligation** from the salary before applying the multiplier. TCL's highest multiplier is **27x**.
*   **Bajaj Finserv's Segmentation:** BFL utilizes a highly complex multiplier grid based on risk segmentation and salary band, peaking at **24x** for the best profiles with a Home Loan track. The lowest general multiplier is 10x.
*   **Lenders Excluding Multipliers:** **Piramal Finance** explicitly states **"No Multiplier Calculation"**, relying solely on FOIR (up to 75%) and risk profile. **MAS Finance** also uses **"Only FOIR basis approval"**. **InCred**, for self-employed non-professionals, uses a **Max EMI Logic** based on Average Bank Balance (ABB) rather than a simple NTH multiplier.

#### 3. Cash/Cheque Salary Acceptance

Direct salary credit is overwhelmingly preferred, and several key lenders explicitly reject or impose strict conditions on non-direct modes like cash or cheque payments, or even IMPS transfers.

*   **Explicitly Disallowed (Bajaj Finserv):** BFL explicitly rejects non-direct modes: "Salary credits in bank statement should be only through direct mode and **not cash/cheque**".
*   **Strict Conditions (WeRize):** WeRize requires salary to be via **Bank transfer (NEFT/RTGS/IMPS)**. If the payment is via IMPS mode, the employer must be a **PVT LTD company** and **PF deduction should be there**. This implies that cash or cheque payments are not accepted.
*   **Implicitly Disallowed (Poonawala):** PFL explicitly states **"Cash Salary not allowed"** for Cat E employees. Salary must reflect as a credit in the bank statement.
*   **Focus on Documentation (ABCL, Piramal):** Some policies emphasize confirming income via external proofs if salary slips are missing, such as Form 16, IT Returns, or PF/OMC confirmation. While this might allow documentation for an unusual payment structure, they still rely on verifiable bank credits or official tax documents, rather than raw cash deposits.

***
**Analogy:** The interplay between FOIR and the Multiplier is like planning a long road trip with a budget.

**FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio)** is your **Fuel Gauge**. It measures how much of your monthly income is already spent on fixed costs (EMIs, rent). Even if you have a massive engine (high multiplier), if your fuel gauge is already showing 90% usage (high FOIR), the bank won't let you start a new journey, fearing you'll run out of gas mid-way.

The **Multiplier** is the **Size of your Gas Tank**. If your salary is high, the multiplier (e.g., 27x) allows you to draw a large loan (a huge tank). However, Kotak checks if your tank is already partially empty *before* calculating the size (NTH - Obligation \* Multiplier), while Tata Capital calculates the full tank size *first* (NTH \* Multiplier), and then uses the FOIR rule to check if the new EMI fits into your remaining safe spending capacity (remaining fuel).
